NexusCompress · Compress image for WhatsApp without losing quality

WhatsApp recompresses photos sent as images — often aggressively. Pre-compress at a controlled quality, then send as a Document to keep text and detail readable.

Why WhatsApp degrades your photos

WhatsApp targets roughly 1600 × 1200 px at 65–75% JPEG quality when you send as a photo. A 5 MB camera JPEG can shrink to under 100 KB — destroying detail in documents, menus, and product shots.

Photo vs Document in WhatsApp

  • Send as Photo — WhatsApp recompresses automatically.
  • Send as Document — your file is sent unchanged. Best for scans and quality-sensitive images.

Best approach: Pre-compress to under 1 MB, then send as a Document so WhatsApp does not recompress further.

Recommended settings

  • Format: JPEG
  • Quality: 80–85%
  • Max width: 1600 px
  • Target size: 300–600 KB for photos; under 200 KB for document scans

How to compress in NexusCompress

  1. Open NexusCompress.
  2. Select preset Email — JPEG, 1200px wide, or Custom with 1600 px max width.
  3. Set quality to 80%.
  4. Drop images and download.
  5. Send the file as a Document in WhatsApp.

Document scans via WhatsApp

For contracts, certificates, or ID copies, use UAE Portal Presets at 500 KB or set a custom target under 500 KB. Send as Document.

Batch compress for groups

Drop an entire folder, set max width 1600 px at 80% quality, and download as ZIP before sharing to a WhatsApp group.

Frequently asked questions

Why does WhatsApp blur my photos?

WhatsApp recompresses images sent as photos. Pre-compress yourself and send as a Document to avoid this.

What is the best size for WhatsApp?

Max width 1600 px, JPEG at 80% quality — typically 300–500 KB per image.

How do I send high-quality images on WhatsApp?

Tap the attachment icon and choose Document instead of Gallery/Photo.

Does NexusCompress upload my images?

No. All compression happens in your browser — images never leave your device.
